1 / 9 Project Overview

Epigenomic Regulation of Tail Regeneration in Deroceras laeve

Whole-genome bisulfite sequencing + RNA-seq + gene regulatory network integration

Central question: How does DNA methylation regulate gene expression during tissue regeneration in an invertebrate?

Central answer: It doesn't regulate expression directly. Instead, methylation marks network topology -- it targets genes based on their position in co-expression networks, not to control their transcriptional output. The classical "hypermethylation = silencing" rule is entirely absent.

The Organism

Deroceras laeve (gray field slug) is a gastropod mollusk -- a non-model invertebrate that can regenerate its tail after amputation. This is only the second gastropod whole methylome ever published and the first study of DNA methylation dynamics during regeneration in any invertebrate.

2 / 9 Key Findings

Finding 1: MI = 0 -- Methylation Direction Does NOT Predict Expression Direction

Result: Mutual information between methylation direction (hyper/hypo) and expression direction (up/down) is MI = 0.00009 bits (p = 0.55). The classical "hypermethylation = gene silencing" rule is entirely absent genome-wide.

What is Mutual Information?

MI measures how much knowing one variable reduces uncertainty about another. If methylation direction predicted expression direction, MI would be high. We found MI = 0.00009 bits -- essentially zero. Knowing whether a gene is hypermethylated tells you absolutely nothing about whether it is up- or down-regulated.

Statistical Details

MI(X; Y) = ∑∑ p(x,y) x log2[ p(x,y) / (p(x) x p(y)) ]

Our contingency table:
                Up-regulated    Down-regulated
Hypermethylated    ~25%            ~25%
Hypomethylated     ~25%            ~25%

MI = 0.00009 bits, p = 0.55 (10,000 permutations)
Maximum possible: 1 bit  |  Observed/Max: 0.009%

Why This Matters

The "methylation = silencing" model has been the default assumption for decades. Our finding means:

  1. Hypermethylated genes are equally likely to go UP or DOWN
  2. The classical model is entirely absent in this system
  3. Methylation is doing something else -- marking network topology, not controlling expression
  4. This is not noise -- methylation targeting is highly non-random (entropy H/Hmax = 0.80, clustering at 73x expected)
MI Analysis. MI = 0.00009 bits (p=0.55), confirming complete statistical independence.
Quadrant Density. Genes distribute uniformly across all four quadrants.

Finding 2: Intergenic Methylation Is the Primary Regulatory Mechanism

63% of DMPs fall in intergenic and intronic regions -- NOT at promoters. DMPs cluster at 73x the expected rate within 1 kb, forming coherent regulatory domains.

In mammals, methylation is a promoter story. In D. laeve, this model is structurally inapplicable: 91.6% ICP promoters, only ~12% of DMPs at promoters, intergenic DMPs show the strongest expression correlations.

Intergenic methylation changes likely mark distal regulatory elements: enhancers, silencers, and insulators. Supported by MethNet (Nature Communications, 2024), H3K4me3 at intergenic regions (Genes & Development, 2025), and enhancer methylation review (IJC, 2026).

Finding 3: Methylation Is a Binary Switch, Not a Rheostat

No dose-response relationship. Cochran-Armitage: z = 1.009, p = 0.31. Whether a gene has 1 DMP at 10% or 50 DMPs at 60%, the probability of being DE is the same.

Binary Switch vs Rheostat

A rheostat = more methylation, more effect. A binary switch = presence matters, amount doesn't. Our data shows binary switch: methylation flags genes for network-level regulation, but effect depends on network context, not methylation magnitude.

Binary Switch. DE rate flat across methylation bins (p=0.31).
ML Dosage Models. Neither linear nor quadratic models improve prediction.

Finding 4: The Hub Gene Paradox

Hub genes are methylated but NOT DE. Blue hub genes: DMR OR = 1.97 (padj = 0.033), 0 DE. kME vs methylation: ρ = +0.176, p = 0.007. Contradicts the buffering hypothesis.

Hubs are MORE methylated because of their topological importance, but the methylation signal does not translate to expression change. Methylation marks network position, not expression level.

Finding 7: TFs Are Methylated but Expression-Protected

REVISED (Mar 2026): DeepTFactor CNN-based TF identification (2,217 TFs from 73,425 proteins) shows TFs are ENRICHED for DMPs (OR = 1.607, p = 4.32e-13) and DMRs (OR = 1.645, p = 3.33e-06). The old keyword-based finding (OR = 0.77 depleted) was an artifact of incomplete TF identification.

The paradox: 14.8% of TFs have DMPs (vs 9.8% non-TFs), yet 0/6 DE TFs are methylated. Methylation targets TFs but does NOT change their expression. Top methylated TFs: ZFP2 (30 DMPs), Mafb (25), ATF2 (18), Hr96 (18). DE TFs (Scrt1, REPTOR-BP, Y-box, sox8, CEBPA, Catenin) achieve expression changes WITHOUT methylation.

TE-DMP Genomic Context (New)

51.1% of DMPs overlap TEs (9,583/18,754). TE-DMPs are enriched for intergenic regions (OR = 1.305, p = 3.10e-17). DNA transposons dominate (53%), followed by LINEs (36%). 66.5% of DMRs overlap TEs.

Precedent

Used by ENCODE, Roadmap Epigenomics, BLUEPRINT. Recently applied to non-model organisms via DMRichR FAIRification (Bioinformatics Advances, 2025).

Key Insight

The Weber HCP/ICP/LCP classification has never been applied to an invertebrate genome before. Planarians (champion regenerators) have ZERO methylation. Zebrafish methylation is STABLE during regeneration. Our finding of dynamic methylation during invertebrate regeneration is unique.

Mathematical Formulas

Mutual Information (MI)

MI(X; Y) = sum sum p(x,y) * log2[ p(x,y) / (p(x) * p(y)) ]

X in {hypermethylated, hypomethylated}
Y in {upregulated, downregulated}

Our result: MI = 0.00009 bits (p = 0.55)
Maximum possible: 1 bit  |  Observed/Max: 0.009%

Shannon Entropy

H = -sum p_i * log2(p_i)
H_max = log2(n modules)
Normalized: H/H_max = 0.80  (1.0 = uniform, 0.0 = one module)

Cochran-Armitage Trend Test

Tests: P(DE) increases with methylation magnitude?
z = 1.009, p = 0.31  ->  No trend. Binary switch.

Fisher's Exact Test (Module Enrichment)

              DMPs    No DMPs
Module genes |  a  |    b    |
Other genes  |  c  |    d    |

OR = (a*d) / (b*c)
TF enrichment (DeepTFactor): OR = 1.607, p = 4.32e-13
Old keyword result (superseded): OR = 0.77, p = 5.87e-5

Kimura 2-Parameter → Million Years

Time(My) = Kimura_div / (2 * 2.2e-9 * 1e6)
Gastropod rate = 2.2e-9 per site per year
1% Kimura ~ 2.3 My  |  10% ~ 22.7 My  |  50% ~ 113.6 My

DSS DMP Detection (Beta-Binomial)

X_ij ~ BetaBinomial(n_ij, pi_ij, phi_j)
Test: arcsin(sqrt(pi_control)) - arcsin(sqrt(pi_amputated))
DMP: FDR < 0.05 (BH)  |  DMR: areaStat = sum |Wald| in region
